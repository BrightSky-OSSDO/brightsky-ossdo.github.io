Frequently Asked Questions

How much do your products cost? I can't find a pricing page.

All our products are free and open-source, which means you can access and use them without any cost. We believe in providing accessible and high-quality software to our users, which is why we don't have a pricing page.

How do I contact you for support?

We offer multiple avenues for support. You can tag us on X, send us an email, or post your queries on our official subreddit. We strive to provide timely and helpful assistance to our users through these channels.

Do you have a physical store where I can see the products?

As our products are software and online tools, we do not have a physical store. Since our offerings are digital in nature, there are no physical items to showcase or sell. However, you can access and explore our products directly through our website.

To unsubscribe from our newsletter or marketing emails, simply send us an email expressing your preference not to receive our release updates. We respect your choices and will promptly remove you from our mailing list upon receiving your request.

When will BrightSky OS 1.0 be released?

BrightSky OS, our flagship operating system, is currently in the beta phase, offering limited features and known issues. We anticipate the full release, version 1.0, to be available either later in 2024 or sometime in 2025. We're continuously working to refine and enhance the OS to ensure a stable and feature-rich experience for our users.

Are your websites safe?

Yes, our websites are safe to use. While it's possible that some of our websites may not have SSL certificates, it doesn't mean they are inherently unsafe. We prioritize user safety and take necessary precautions to ensure the security of our platforms and user data.

Why are your products free and open-source?

We believe in the power of open-source software and the principles of inclusivity and accessibility. By offering our products for free, we ensure that everyone, regardless of their financial status, can access and benefit from them. We strive to eliminate the barriers that restrict access to cutting-edge technology and promote a world where technological advancement is a universal right. Additionally, we do not display ads on our websites as we want to provide a clean and user-friendly experience without any unnecessary distractions.

You said that your products are open-source. Where can I find the source code?

We are actively working on making the source code for our products available to the public. We plan to post the source code on GitHub within the next few months. Once the repositories are set up, we will provide links to each repository on the appropriate webpage, allowing users to access and explore the source code.

Will BrightSky OS 1.0 introduce any new file extensions?

Yes, BrightSky OS 1.0 will introduce several new file extensions that are exclusive to the operating system. Some of these include the .bright extension, which stands for BrightSky OS Executable and represents a program file, and the .mv extension, which stands for Motion Video and represents a video file. These are just a few examples, and there are more unique file extensions that will be introduced with the release of BrightSky OS 1.0.

Why is USIARC not loading? I received a 403 error when I attempted to navigate to it.

Unfortunately, the original domain where it was hosted became inaccessible due to unknown reasons. We are actively working on creating a new version of USIARC on Fandom, which we hope will provide a more reliable and accessible platform for space-related information.

What does the "OSSDO" in your company name stand for?

"OSSDO" stands for Open Source Software Development Organization. It represents our commitment to developing and promoting open-source software solutions.
